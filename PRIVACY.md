# Privacy Policy — Tabby

**Last updated:** July 5, 2026

**Privacy policy URL (for Chrome Web Store):**
https://github.com/yanovian/chrome-ext-tabby/blob/master/PRIVACY.md

## Summary

**Tabby does not collect your data.** There is no account, no analytics server, and no
cloud AI. Everything Tabby knows about your browsing stays **on your device**, inside
your browser.

Tabby reads **tab titles, URLs, and optionally visible page text** to understand what
you are looking at and react as your browser cat. This data is processed **locally**
and is **never uploaded** anywhere.

## What Tabby reads

To care for Tabby and classify pages locally, the extension may read:

- **Tab title** and **URL** while you browse
- **Visible page text** (a short snippet from the page you are on), when enabled in
  settings — **on by default**, because that is why most people install Tabby

Tabby uses a **content script** to show the floating cat on pages and, when enabled,
extract a limited text snippet from the page you are viewing. Classification runs with
local rules on your device. **Speech lines** are generated by a bundled local model
(Transformers.js + ONNX) in an offscreen document — still fully on-device, with no
upload. No page content is sent to any server.

## What Tabby stores locally

All data lives in your browser:

- **Cat state** — hunger, happiness, stress, energy, age, and mood (IndexedDB)
- **Browsing observations** — title, URL, hostname, optional text snippet, duration,
  and local category (IndexedDB)
- **Memories** — topic summaries Tabby can recall in conversation (IndexedDB)
- **Settings** — your preferences (`chrome.storage.local`)

You can export or delete this data from the settings popup.

## What we do not do

- We do **not** operate a backend that receives your browsing data
- We do **not** use cloud AI or send pages to third-party inference services
- We do **not** sell data — there is nothing to sell, because we never receive it
- We do **not** use remote code at runtime (including speech — the model ships with the extension)

## Permissions

| Permission | Why |
|------------|-----|
| `tabs` | Know which tab is active and read its title/URL |
| `storage` | Save cat state and settings locally |
| `alarms` | Gentle minute-by-minute care simulation while you browse |
| `offscreen` | Run the bundled local speech model without blocking the service worker |
| `<all_urls>` (host) | Show Tabby on pages and optionally read visible text locally |
