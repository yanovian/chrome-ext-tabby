# How to use Tabby

A cat lives in your browser. She reacts to the pages you visit (tab title and URL only). Everything stays on your device.

## 1. Browse with Tabby

Install Tabby and open any page. She appears in the corner while you browse.

Drag her anywhere you like. She may speak up now and then, or stay quiet if she is sleepy.

![Tabby on a page while you browse](./screenshots/screenshot-2.jpg)

## 2. Tap Tabby to interact

Click the cat to open her menu. Pick an action — **Pet**, **Play**, **Feed**, or **What's up?**

She answers in a speech bubble. Tap outside the menu or press **×** to close it.

![Tabby's care menu and speech bubble](./screenshots/screenshot-1.jpg)

![After petting Tabby](./screenshots/screenshot-3.jpg)

## 3. Open settings

Click the **Tabby icon** in the toolbar.

From here you can:

- Set **do not disturb** so Tabby stays hidden on every tab for a while
- **Show** or **hide** Tabby on this page or on every page
- Set **quiet hours** so unprompted speech stays off at night
- Turn **local AI page classification** on or off (title and URL only, never page body text)

![Tabby settings — hide and privacy](./screenshots/screenshot-4.jpg)

![Show Tabby again after hiding her everywhere](./screenshots/screenshot-5.jpg)

---

**That's it.** Browse → tap Tabby → adjust settings. No account. No cloud. All local.
